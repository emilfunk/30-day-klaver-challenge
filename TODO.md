This project will create a 30 day piano and music theory challenge The progression perfectly reflects a beginner-to-intermediate music theory journey. We will break the 30 days down into  **four logical phases** . Every day will feature exactly **10 bite-sized exercises** that we can format onto two A4 pages, gradually building her sight-reading and theory skills.

Here is the structured 30-Day Blueprint for Ilina's Challenge:

### **Phase 1: The Absolute Basics (Days 1–7)**

*Goal: Familiarize Ilina with the piano topography, rhythmic fundamentals, and the Grand Staff.*

* **Day 1: The Piano Keyboard.** Identifying white keys, finding Middle C, and the musical alphabet.
* **Day 2: The Grand Staff & Clefs.** Drawing and understanding the Treble Clef, Bass Clef, and bar lines.
* **Day 3: Basic Rhythms.** Quarter, half, dotted-half, and whole notes. Music math (adding beats).
* **Day 4: Rests & Time Signatures.** Understanding 4/4 and 3/4 time, matching rests to their note equivalents.
* **Day 5: Treble Clef Notes.** Lines (EGBDF) and spaces (FACE). Note identification exercises.
* **Day 6: Bass Clef Notes.** Lines (GBDFA) and spaces (ACEG). Note identification exercises.
* **Day 7: Phase 1 Review.** Mixed treble/bass note naming, rhythm counting, and keyboard mapping.

### **Phase 2: Intervals, Melodies & Accidentals (Days 8–14)**

*Goal: Move from single notes to reading spatial distances, basic sight-reading, and introducing black keys.*

* **Day 8: Steps and Skips (2nds and 3rds).** Identifying spatial distances on the staff and the keyboard.
* **Day 9: Middle C Position (Right Hand).** Sight-reading short 3-to-4 note melodic patterns.
* **Day 10: Middle C Position (Left Hand).** Sight-reading short patterns in the bass clef.
* **Day 11: Accidentals (Sharps & Flats).** Identifying and drawing sharp and flat symbols, finding them on the keys.
* **Day 12: Natural Signs & Enharmonics.** Canceling out sharps/flats, understanding that C# is Db.
* **Day 13: Eighth Notes.** Subdividing the beat. Rhythm clapping and counting exercises.
* **Day 14: Phase 2 Review.** Sight-reading small snippets with accidentals and eighth notes.

### **Phase 3: Chords and Key Signatures (Days 15–21)**

*Goal: Introduce harmony, scales, and reading multiple notes at once.*

* **Day 15: Major Scales.** The formula for a major scale (Whole-Whole-Half-Whole-Whole-Whole-Half) starting on C.
* **Day 16: Intervals of 4ths and 5ths.** Identifying larger leaps on the staff for sight-reading.
* **Day 17: Building Triads.** Stacking 3rds. Identifying the root, 3rd, and 5th of a chord.
* **Day 18: Major Chords.** Recognizing and building C, F, and G major chords on the staff.
* **Day 19: Minor Chords.** Altering major chords to minor (A minor, D minor, E minor).
* **Day 20: Key Signatures (The Basics).** Understanding what the sharps/flats next to the clef mean (F Major & G Major).
* **Day 21: Phase 3 Review.** Identifying drawn chords, sight-reading simple LH chord / RH melody exercises.

### **Phase 4: The Circle of Fifths & Fluent Sight-Reading (Days 22–30)**

*Goal: Solidify advanced beginner theory, dynamic markings, and integrate everything into actual reading.*

* **Day 22: Introduction to the Circle of Fifths.** Understanding the layout and the sharp keys (G, D, A, E).
* **Day 23: The Circle of Fifths (Flat Keys).** Mapping the flat keys (F, Bb, Eb, Ab).
* **Day 24: Relative Minors.** Using the Circle of Fifths to find relative minor keys.
* **Day 25: Dynamics & Articulation.** Matching symbols (Staccato, Legato, Forte, Piano, Crescendo).
* **Day 26: Sixteenth Notes.** Advanced rhythm math and counting.
* rt snippets applying the key signatures.
* **Day 28: Sight-Reading in F Major & Bb Major.** 10 short snippets applying the flat key signatures.
* **Day 29: Chord Inversions (Preview).** A gentle introduction to chords played in different shapes.
* **Day 30: The Grand Finale!** A mix of rhythm math, naming chords, applying the Circle of Fifths, and reading a final mini-piece.

### **Anatomy of the Daily 10 Exercises**

For each day, the 10 exercises will be highly varied so Ilina doesn't get bored. They will include:

1. **True/False** or **Multiple Choice** questions.
2. **Note Naming** (writing the letter under the staff).
3. **Drawing** (drawing clefs, notes, or sharp/flat symbols on the staff).
4. **Music Math** (e.g., Quarter note + Half note = ? beats).
5. **Keyboard Mapping** (shading in the correct piano keys).
6. **Sight-Reading Mini-Tests** (short, 1-bar rhythmic or melodic tasks to play).
